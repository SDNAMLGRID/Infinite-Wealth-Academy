## What is the Retirement Income Gap?

The retirement income gap is the shortfall between the income you will actually need to sustain your lifestyle in retirement and the income your current savings will generate.

This gap is one of the biggest financial risks most families don't see coming [3]. It occurs because there is a fundamental difference between merely *saving* your money and strategically *positioning* it [3]. If your assets are not positioned correctly, you remain exposed to inflation, market volatility, and unforeseen life events.

## Risks You Might Be Missing

When we conduct a complimentary Financial Needs Analysis (FNA) for our clients [4], we frequently uncover several blind spots that contribute to the retirement income gap. Ask yourself:

* **Do you know what happens to your retirement accounts after leaving a job?** Leaving old accounts unmanaged or improperly transferred can result in lost growth potential or unexpected tax penalties [3].
* **Are you underinsured?** Many families are severely underinsured, meaning an unexpected health crisis or loss of a breadwinner could instantly drain their retirement savings [3].

## A Smarter Approach: Strategic Protection

We believe that financial protection should be simple to understand [3]. To close the retirement income gap and build generational confidence, you must move beyond just saving and move toward strategic protection [1].

Our approach focuses on four key solutions that provide long-term clarity:

1. **Income Protection:** Safeguarding your ability to generate income, ensuring your family's assets are guarded no matter what happens [3, 4].
2. **Living Benefits:** Utilizing insurance policies that you don't have to die to use, protecting your retirement savings from being wiped out by sudden medical expenses [3].
3. **Tax-Advantaged Strategies:** Positioning your money in ways that minimize your tax burden, allowing you to keep more of what you earn during retirement [3].
4. **Retirement Income Flexibility:** Designing a plan that adapts to life's inevitable changes, rather than locking you into a rigid structure [3, 5].
